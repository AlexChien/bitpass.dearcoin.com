Bitpass FAQ

Do iOS users need to set up iCloud?

Yes. Bitpass uses iCloud as the primary encrypted backup method, so please turn on iCloud to back up your keys.

How do I back up my keys?

Backups are automatic. When you create or modify a key (such as changing a label), your keys are automatically backed up again.

Are backups immediate?

Upon modifications, Bitpass will automatically back up your keys. However, there may be a delay backing up to iCloud, depending on network circumstances.

Can I delete my backups?

Yes. However, we recommend that you don't use iCloud to manage your keys. Rather, use Bitpass if you would like to delete any keys.

Are my backups secure?

Definitely. Your backups are stored on iCloud using Apple's secure backup methods. We use the same ECDSA cryptography techniques as Bitcoin to generate your keys, PBKDF2 to encrypt your master password, and then apply AES256 (the highest strength AES available) encryption on top of that. Other than you, no one will be able to access your data.

Does Bitpass store user data on its servers?

No. Bitpass is a client side application, and does not rely on any central servers.

How do I recover my data if my phone gets lost or stolen?

If you lose your iPhone, just open iCloud and log in with your same Apple ID on your new phone. After you install Bitpass, it will automatically load your data from your iCloud backup.
